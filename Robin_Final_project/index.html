Vegan Restaurants

scroll down and search any Vegan places listed below

Peacefood, East 11

There’s a wide menu of vegan eats at this friendly Union Square-adjacent spot, which has a bakery area for more casual, quick occasions.

Blossom on Ninth

Co-founders Ronen Seri and Pamela Elizabeth opened up this Chelsea vegan place in a Ninth Avenue townhouse in 2005, serving an eclectic spread of dishes, like a tofu BLT or risotto croquettes with buffalo sauce.

The Cinnamon Snail

Started as a vegan food truck, the Cinnamon Snail expanded with a brick-and-mortar outpost inside the Pennsy, the food hall near Madison Square Garden that offers up some of the area’s better dining options for a quick meal (other vendors include Pat LeFrieda and Little Beet).

Candle 79

Candle 79 serves up solid vegan options with nods to a variety of cuisines, utilizing fresh, well-sourced produce in dishes like raw zucchini enchiladas, wild mushroom crepes, and za’atar roasted cauliflower. For more variety, try the mezze platter or a customizable market plate.

Hangawi

In a hushed, ceremonial-feeling setting amid the energetic Koreatown, diners eat shoeless and tucked into seating situated close to the floor at Hangawi, a Korean vegan restaurant serving healthy, often light dishes. Try the stone bowl rice creations, clay pot silky tofu dishes, and unusual salads, like kale, broccoli kimchi, beets, and avocado.

Ladybird East

The new menu is comprised of interesting small plates like kale with pickled daikon, radish, puffed rice, and truffle vinaigrette or fondue, which swaps cheese for tapioca, garlic, and white wine. Cocktails were created Mother of Pearl and Post Office Whiskey Bar vets.

Spicy Moon Vegetarian Szechuan

Classic Sichuan dishes get a vegan twist at Spicy Moon, open in a small, cozy space tucked away in the East Village. Options include General Tso’s mushroom and vegetable wontons in chili oil, with bigger plates offering vegetables, tofu, eggplant, or potato in dry pepper, dry pot, and kung pao style. Pair it all with boozy milk tea or a rice wine martini.
